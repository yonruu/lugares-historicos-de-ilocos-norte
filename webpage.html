About Us

Welcome to our Travel Webpage, where we invite you to embark on a journey through the enchanting beauty of Ilocos Norte. Nestled in the northern part of the Philippines, Ilocos Norte is a province rich in history and adorned with breathtaking landscapes. Our mission is to bring you closer to the heart of this cultural treasure trove, allowing you to discover the charm of its historical sites and awe-inspiring tourist spots.



In Ilocos Norte, history comes alive as you explore the centuries-old architecture of Paoay Church, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Feel the echoes of the past at the Malacañang of the North, once the official residence of the Marcos family. As you traverse the streets of Vigan, a UNESCO-listed city, let the cobblestone roads guide you through Spanish colonial architecture and horse-drawn carriage rides. Nature lovers will find solace in the scenic landscapes of Kapurpurawan Rock Formation and the serene Paoay Lake. Join us on this virtual expedition to Ilocos Norte, where each site tells a story, and every moment is a step back in time.

Paoay Church

The Saint Augustine Church (Spanish: Iglesia de San Agustín de Paoay), commonly known as the Paoay Church, is a Roman Catholic church in the municipality of Paoay, Ilocos Norte in the Philippines. Completed in 1710, the church is famous for its distinct architecture highlighted by the enormous buttresses on the sides and back of the building. It is declared as a National Cultural Treasure by the Philippine government in 1973 and a UNESCO World Heritage Site under the collective group of Baroque Churches of the Philippines in 1993.
